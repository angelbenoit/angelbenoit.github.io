Artificial Sweetener Cancer or Myth?

What are the claims?

The claim is that consuming artificial sweetener can give you cancer. Why so?
Artificial sweetener contains chemicals like sucralose or aspartame that are meant to replicate the same taste of regular sugar without actually gaining the calories.



Typically, a can or bottle of soda will contain around 100 calories and 40-80 grams of sugar. In one serving of soda, you will have exceeded your recommended daily sugar intake (which should be less than 40). Lots of soda brands have versions of their drink like diet coke and coke zero. In addition, there are many brands that sell artificial sweetener in little packets. Access to artificial sugar is as easy as regular sugar. A lot of stores sell them. Additionally, there is a lot of advertisements going towards them, such as weight loss and healthy eating programs.

Why People Choose Artificial Sweeteners


One of the main reasons people consume artificial sweetener over regular sugar is the health benefits. Consuming a lot of regular sugar can lead to diabetes and weight gain, as it contains calories. In fact, artificial sweetener is sweeter than regular sugar. This can make loosing weight a lot easier. The process of losing weight not only involves exercise, but it's also the food that you eat. A lot of people have trouble to cut the sugar out of their diet. However, with artificial sweetener, they don't have to. Seems too good to be true, has no calories and tastes better?

In addition, diabetics can consume sweets with it. Some people might have a difficult time transitioning from a life without diabetes to a life with it, especially if they are not allowed to have any sugar. Artificial sweetener solves this problem. Since it does not raise the glucose level, diabetics can enjoy themselves with sweets.
